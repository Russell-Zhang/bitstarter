The Problem

Most of us have expressed our emotions and thoughts on the Internet. It might have been a quick comment like: "The new Startrek movie rocks!", a review of the smart phone you just bought, or, an analysis of the economic impact of the latest federal tax cut.

To express these thoughts and feelings, we had to sign in to a dozen different websites or applications that are usually specialized and isolated. This creates a problem: after a while we forgot that we had shared some excellent tips on, say, the best ways to take travel pictures; nor could we recall the user names and passwords of those 20 different websites we registered on. And the worst thing is, a lot of the people around the world who would have deeply appreciated our writings are not even going to see our article, not to mention exchanging their thoughts with us on this topic.

A Better Way Ahead

Wouldn't it be nice if there is a portal where we could easily see all our writings, short or long, on various topics ranging from food to music, to the latest books we read? And would it be wonderful if we can see how much interest our writings have generated among other users? In addition, wouldn't it be wonderful if we can be financially rewarded because people bought a book, a camera, or a tablet computer based on an excellent review we wrote?

That's what ELICI is about, you simply write something on a given topic whenever you like, it's stored in realtime and ready for your visit at any time. As soon as you choose to publish this writing, people with similar interests would be able to read and evaluate it. The positive feedbacks from other users would help you rise through the ranks in our online community, you could be regarded a Rookie, Veteran, Champion, Expert, Master, or Guru on any given subject, depending on how well your writing is received by the audience. Better yet, Your ranks would open doors to additional features on our website, such as premium contents contributed by industry professionals, and free admission to our seminars.

If, instead of making a comment, you want to perform some quick research on a given topic. Say, "what does everyone think of the investment potentials of Apple's stock right now?" Or, "what's widely considered the best beginner's acoustic guitar tutorials out there?" To find the answer, you can simply navigate to the topic through our intuitive knowledge tree. Once you select a certain branch, the most popular writings on that topic are immediately displayed in order or their popularity. You can also search by keywords or tags, or simply ask other users a question. Again, all the search results and user responses will be automatically sorted by evaluations from users just like yourself. You immediately see the most popular opinions on a given subject, just like consulting with a group of close friends. No longer do you have to collect and compare information from different sources on the Internet, and feel bewildered by those often contradicting and highly subjective articles.
